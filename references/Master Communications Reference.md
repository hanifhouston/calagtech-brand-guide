# California AgTech Alliance

## Master Communications Reference Document

---

## 1. Organizational Identity

**Official Name (Formal):** Statewide California AgTech Network, Entrepreneurship & Workforce Programs

**External-Facing Brand Name:** California AgTech Alliance _(Never use an acronym in external communications.)_

**What the Alliance Is**

The California AgTech Alliance is a statewide coordination and backbone initiative designed to align and amplify agricultural technology, workforce, and entrepreneurship efforts across California. It exists to reduce fragmentation, improve visibility, and accelerate the movement of innovation, talent, and investment across the state's agricultural ecosystem.

The Alliance does not replace or centralize regional programs. Instead, it provides shared infrastructure—governance, branding, storytelling, convening, and connective tools—that enable regional partners to operate more effectively together.

The Alliance is the foundational project ("Project 1") within a broader eight-project statewide coalition funded through California Jobs First. Its role is to support, integrate, and elevate the work of workforce programs, entrepreneurial support organizations, innovation centers, and regional partners.

**Institutional Context**

- Lead institution and fiscal anchor: UC Agriculture and Natural Resources (UC ANR)
- State funding context: California Jobs First / Regional Investment Initiative
- Operating model: Multi-partner, cross-regional, outcomes-driven coordination platform

---

## 2. Vision

California leads the world as a hub of agricultural and food innovation, where every region is connected, every farmer has access to climate-smart technologies, and every worker has pathways to stable, well-paying careers in the future of agriculture.

_This vision emphasizes scale, equity, and coordination—not innovation in isolation—and frames California's agricultural strength as something that depends on connectivity across regions, institutions, and communities._

---

## 3. Mission

To unify California's agricultural regions through a connected and innovative ag and food ecosystem. California AgTech Alliance scales equitable access to good-paying jobs, entrepreneurial opportunity, and climate-smart leadership by creating shared platforms, targeted investment, and cross-regional collaboration.

---

## 4. Core Values

These values guide not only programs and partnerships, but also how the Alliance communicates.

- **Connection as Infrastructure** The Alliance prioritizes building durable connections across regions, sectors, and roles. Communications should reinforce alignment, shared purpose, and interdependence rather than spotlighting individual actors in isolation.

- **Collaboration Over Competition** The Alliance exists to reduce duplication and competition between well-intentioned efforts. Messaging should credit partners, emphasize collective impact, and avoid language that implies ownership of outcomes delivered by others.

- **Equity as a Design Principle** Equity is not a downstream outcome; it is a starting point. Communications should reflect the lived realities of farmworkers, small farmers, and underserved communities without using deficit framing or performative language.

- **Practical Impact** The Alliance measures success through jobs created, skills gained, businesses advanced, and systems improved. Communications should favor concrete outcomes and use-cases over abstract ambition.

- **Stewardship of Public Investment** As a publicly funded initiative, the Alliance communicates with accountability. Claims should be supportable, timelines explicit, and outcomes clearly tied to public benefit.

---

## 5. Tone of Voice

The California AgTech Alliance voice is clear, grounded, and facilitative.

**Primary Characteristics**

- **Connector-oriented:** Focused on alignment, coordination, and shared effort
- **Plainspoken:** Accessible to non-technical audiences without oversimplifying
- **Credible:** Informed by research, industry input, and on-the-ground experience
- **Respectful:** Acknowledges regional leadership, expertise, and lived experience

**What to Avoid**

- Promotional or superlative language
- Over-branding or self-congratulatory framing
- Jargon that obscures meaning or excludes key audiences
- Language that implies the Alliance "delivers" outcomes that are in fact delivered by partners

## 6. Target Audiences

### **Primary**

**Entrepreneurs & Startups** — Innovators developing agricultural technology solutions who need access to business development, technology validation, customers, and funding. Key offerings include entrepreneurship programs, innovation grants, a network of test beds, network navigation and regulatory support, and a network of investors.

**Farmers & Ag Operations** — Agricultural operations seeking solutions for labor, climate, and cost challenges. Key offerings include validated technologies emerging from entrepreneurship programming and skilled workers from workforce programming.

**Workers & Learners** — Students, farmworkers, and career changers seeking hands-on experience, economic mobility, and accessible pathways into agtech careers. Key offerings include workforce programs such as the Farm Robotics Challenge and Academy, FARMS Field Days, DUCC drone certification workshops, and AWDP training, certifications, and paid internships.

**Industry & Policy Leaders** — Sector leaders in agriculture, technology, and public policy. Key offerings include an annual statewide convening, quarterly cross-regional working groups, and policymaker field visits.

**Global Economic Development & Investment Community** — Venture capital, philanthropy, and impact investors. Key offerings include validated technologies emerging from entrepreneurship programming and a startup directory.


## 7. Core Messages

### Core Alliance Narrative (Internal Reference)

The California AgTech Alliance is California's statewide platform for connecting agricultural innovation, workforce development, and entrepreneurship across regions. Led by UC ANR and regional partners, the Alliance aligns programs, investment, and infrastructure to reduce fragmentation, expand access, and accelerate the adoption of climate-smart agricultural solutions. By connecting people, places, and pathways, the Alliance strengthens regional economies and builds a more resilient agricultural future for California.

_This narrative should anchor all communications, regardless of audience or channel._

### Audience-Specific Framing

**For entrepreneurs and startups:** Stop building in a vacuum. The Alliance connects you to real farms, real feedback, and the partners who can help you scale solutions that actually get adopted.

**For farmers agricultural operations:** Your operations run on skilled workers and proven technology. The Alliance delivers both -- a pipeline of trained talent and a network of field-validated innovations ready for adoption at scale.

**For students:** The Alliance connects you to hands-on experiences -- robotics competitions, field days, internships, and professional networks across California -- that turn what you're studying into a career you can see.

**For farmworkers:** You already know agriculture. The Alliance connects you to the training, credentials, and employers that turn your experience into advancement -- without leaving the industry you know.

**For career changers:** You don't need an agricultural background to build a career in AgTech. The Alliance connects you to accessible training, clear credentials, and employers hiring for the skills you're building.

**For the economic development and investment community:** California's AgTech ecosystem is coordinated and grounded in real agricultural needs. The Alliance is the entry point.

**For industry and policy leaders:** Disconnected efforts limit impact. The Alliance aligns workforce, entrepreneurship, and technology programs so innovation reaches the field.

**For partners:** Focus on delivering programs. The Alliance handles the coordination, visibility, and shared infrastructure.

## 8. Program Structure

The California AgTech Alliance is organized around three integrated program areas that together define the scope of the Alliance's work and communications responsibilities. These are not separate programs competing for attention. They are interdependent functions that reinforce one another and should be communicated as parts of a single system.

### A. Workforce

**Purpose** The Workforce pillar focuses on building a statewide pipeline of skilled AgTech talent by aligning training, education, and career pathways across regions. Its role within the Alliance is to ensure that technology adoption and innovation are matched with accessible, industry-aligned workforce development.

**What Falls Under Workforce** Workforce includes coordinated training, exposure, and credentialing efforts that serve:

- Students (high school, community college, university)
- Farmworkers and incumbent workers
- Adult learners and career changers

Programs currently associated with this pillar:

- California Agricultural Workforce Development Program (AWDP)
- Farm Robotics Challenge and Farm Robotics Academy (FRC/FRA)
- Drones Uplift California Communities (DUCC)
- FARMS Ag Technology Field Day Experiences

_These programs are delivered by partners. The Alliance's role is to align, elevate, and connect them rather than operate them directly._

### B. Entrepreneurship

**Purpose** The Entrepreneurship pillar supports the commercialization and scaling of AgTech solutions by connecting startups to capital, expertise, field validation, and market pathways across California's regions. Its function within the Alliance is to shorten the distance between innovation and adoption—particularly for solutions that address labor, climate, and productivity challenges in agriculture.

**What Falls Under Entrepreneurship** Entrepreneurship includes startup-facing programs and tools that support companies at different stages of readiness:

- Early-stage development and validation
- Market readiness and customer discovery
- Field trials, demonstrations, and pilots
- Connections to investors and commercialization partners

Key programs associated with this pillar:

- UC ANR Connect
- Innovation Grant vouchers supporting testing and demonstration

_As with Workforce, delivery is partner-led. The Alliance coordinates visibility, alignment, and access across regions._

### C. Network

**Purpose** The Network pillar is the backbone function of the Alliance. It exists to align people, programs, and resources across regions and to provide shared infrastructure that enables the Workforce and Entrepreneurship pillars to operate at scale. This pillar is what differentiates the Alliance from a collection of programs.

**What Falls Under Network**

- Governance structures and cross-regional working groups
- Industry Advisory Board and Innovation Roadmap
- Statewide convenings and coordination forums
- Investor network development
- Centralized digital hub featuring a statewide network directory, events calendar, test bed map, self-service resources, and intake process for network navigation.
- Unified branding, storytelling tools, and communications infrastructure
- Policy engagement and visibility efforts

_This is the primary area where the Alliance itself is the direct actor._

### How the Three Areas Work Together

The Alliance's effectiveness depends on integration, not balance:

- Workforce efforts supply talent that enables technology adoption
- Entrepreneurship efforts supply solutions that create demand for skilled workers
- Network infrastructure aligns these efforts across regions and over time
