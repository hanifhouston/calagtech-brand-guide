## Brand Overview

Treat the California AgTech Alliance as a statewide connector and backbone initiative. The brand should feel grounded, practical, credible, and focused on aligning people, programs, regions, and resources across agriculture, technology, workforce, entrepreneurship, and investment. 

The tone clear, plainspoken language that emphasizes coordination, shared effort, and practical outcomes. The Alliance should sound confident but measured, with a tone that works for farmers, startups, workers, partners, funders, and policy leaders. #### **Color System**

The color system balances a strong technology presence with warmth, clarity, and a connection to agriculture. Navy and teal provide structure, depth, and credibility, while lime and mint bring energy, freshness, and visibility. Cream and paper tones soften the system, creating space for editorial layouts, long-form content, and more restrained applications. 

#### Typography

Atyp is the brand’s primary type family. **Atyp Display** is used for the largest and most prominent brand moments, including hero headlines, report covers, campaign statements, and signage. **Atyp Text** is used for supporting hierarchy and everyday reading, including subheadings, ledes, body copy, captions, and other editorial uses.

JetBrains Mono is used sparingly for metadata, labels, dates, categories, and technical details. It should function as a structured accent and should not be used for long-form copy or major headlines.

#### Graphic System

- **Motifs**: Individual, standalone design elements extracted from the visual identity. These may include signature shapes, icons, illustration fragments, vector lines, or cropped graphic elements used to seed larger layouts.
- **Graphic Devices**: Flexible visual containers used to organize compositions. These may frame photography, anchor layouts, create emphasis, or guide the viewer’s eye. When used consistently, they help build brand recognition across different materials.
- **Patterns**: Repeating background graphics or tiled vector layouts created from core motifs. Patterns can be used to add texture, fill space, or create visual continuity across branded materials.
